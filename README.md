# Payroll-Case-Study
📌 Overview

This project demonstrates a complete payroll processing workflow using a simulated employee dataset. It includes payroll calculations, supporting data, and professional communication outputs, replicating tasks typically performed by payroll administrators.

The model shows how Microsoft Excel can be used to transform raw employee data into accurate payroll outputs, while the Word document demonstrates how results are communicated clearly and professionally.

---

## Objectives

- To process employee payroll using structured and consistent calculation methods
- To calculate key payroll components including gross pay, tax, National Insurance, pension, and net pay
- To incorporate new joiners and pro-rated salaries into payroll calculations
- To ensure payroll accuracy through validation checks and reconciliation
- To demonstrate professional communication of payroll results through structured email responses

---

## Prerequisites

- Microsoft Excel (2016 or later recommended)
- Microsoft Word

Basic understanding of:
- Excel formulas
- Payroll concepts (tax, NI, pension)
- Professional email communication

---

## Key Features

- Structured payroll dataset with realistic employee data
- End-to-end payroll calculation model
- Integration of new starters and P45 information
- Consistent application of deduction rates
- Supporting datasets for traceability and validation
- Professional payroll communication examples (emails and payslips)

## Workbook Structure

**Payroll_Run** – Main payroll calculations for all employees
**Summary** – Aggregated totals and final payroll figures
**Outputs** – Final net pay values for each employee

**Employee_Master_Data** – Core employee details (names, salaries)
**Starter_P45_Data** – New joiner information and tax details
**Monthly_Changes_Data** – Adjustments such as bonuses or pro-rated salaries
**Payroll_Assumptions_Data** – Standard deduction rates applied

---

## Payroll Methodology

Payroll calculations are based on standard percentage deductions applied to gross pay:

- Income Tax – 35%
- National Insurance – 10%
- Pension – 5%
- Student Loan – 5% (where applicable)

New joiners are handled as follows:
- Full-month employees receive full salary
- Mid-month starters are pro-rated based on days worked
- Bonuses are included in gross pay where applicable

All calculations flow from source data through to final outputs, ensuring consistency and traceability.

---

## Validation & Checks

The model includes key validation steps:

- Verification of employee data against source records
- Confirmation of tax codes and deduction applicability
- Reconciliation of totals across all sheets
- Review of pro-rata calculations for accuracy

These checks ensure payroll accuracy prior to submission.

---

## Communication Outputs

The Word document includes structured payroll communications:

- Approval email prior to submission
- Payroll results summary
- Individual payslip breakdowns
- Key questions and assumptions

This demonstrates how payroll data is translated into clear, professional communication.
